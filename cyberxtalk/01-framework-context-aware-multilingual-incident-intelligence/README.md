# Paper 1: CyberXTalk Framework

## Working Title

**CyberXTalk: A Context-Aware Multilingual Cyber Incident Reporting and Intelligence Framework for Low-Resource Digital Economies**

## Core Idea

This paper presents CyberXTalk as a pre-CERT cyber incident intelligence framework that transforms informal human cybercrime reports into structured, actionable intelligence.

## Research Gap

Existing formal cybersecurity systems such as CERT portals, SIEM/SOAR platforms, and institutional incident-response systems are often designed around structured technical reporting or machine-generated telemetry.

However, ordinary victims and SMEs often describe incidents using informal, incomplete, noisy, and multilingual narratives.

## Contribution

- Pre-CERT positioning for citizen/SME-facing cyber incident reporting.
- Context-aware reporting workflow.
- Multilingual incident intake concept.
- Structured intelligence output for triage and escalation.
- Architecture connecting report intake, classification, evidence capture, severity scoring, and response support.

## Possible Research Questions

1. How can informal human cybercrime narratives be transformed into structured cyber incident intelligence?
2. What architectural components are required for a pre-CERT incident reporting layer in low-resource environments?
3. How can CyberXTalk complement rather than compete with existing cybersecurity response institutions?

## Notes

This paper is the mother framework paper. It should establish the system, architecture, motivation, and pre-CERT positioning.
